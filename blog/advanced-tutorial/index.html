Step 1: Define Your Video Topic and Outline

Start with a clear goal. What do you want your viewer to learn or do by the end of the video?

Example topics: - "How to create a landing page in 30 minutes" - "3 AI tools every content creator should use" - "Why your newsletter isn't growing (and how to fix it)"

Write down: 1. Title: A clear, benefit-driven headline 2. Duration: Aim for 60–90 seconds for social; 2–3 minutes for longer explainers 3. Key points: 3–5 bullet points you want to cover

This outline will guide Copy.ai and keep your script focused.

Step 2: Generate Your Script with Copy.ai

Open Copy.ai and go to Chat or create a new project.

Prompt template:

Write a video script for [Topic]. Length: [X] seconds when spoken at a natural pace (~150 words per minute).

Structure:
- Hook (first 10 seconds): grab attention
- Main points: [list your 3–5 points]
- CTA: what should the viewer do next?

Tone: [conversational / professional / friendly]
Keep sentences short (under 15 words) for easy narration.

Example prompt:

Write a video script for "5 tips for better remote meetings." Length: 90 seconds when spoken at a natural pace (~150 words per minute). Structure: Hook (first 10 seconds), main points (5 tips), CTA. Tone: conversational, friendly. Keep sentences short for easy narration.

Copy.ai will generate a script. Use it as a first draft—you'll refine it in the next step.

Pro tip: If your script is long, use Copy.ai's "Continue" or ask it to "break this into 3 scenes of 30 seconds each" for easier Vidnoz handling.

Step 3: Refine and Format the Script

Before moving to Vidnoz, clean up the script:

  1. Read it aloud — Fix any awkward phrasing or tongue-twisters
  2. Cut filler — Remove "um," "basically," "so" unless needed for tone
  3. Add scene markers (optional) — If your video has multiple sections, add [Scene 1], [Scene 2] so you can split in Vidnoz
  4. Check word count — ~150 words per minute ≈ 2.5 words per second. A 90-second video ≈ 225 words.

Script format for Vidnoz: - One paragraph per scene, or - Use line breaks between logical sections

Ready to produce? Open Vidnoz →

Step 4: Create a Vidnoz Account and Start a New Video

  1. Sign up at Vidnoz (free or paid)
  2. Create a new project — choose Text to Video or AI Video Wizard
  3. Name your project (e.g., "Remote Meeting Tips")

The Text to Video option lets you paste your full script; Vidnoz will auto-generate scenes, avatar placement, and visuals. The AI Video Wizard offers more guided setup if you prefer.

Step 5: Choose Your Avatar and Voice

  1. Select an avatar — Browse the library (1,800+ on free, 1,900+ on paid). Pick one that matches your brand and audience.
  2. Choose a voice — Vidnoz has 890+ voices on free, 2,660+ on paid. Listen to samples; pick a natural-sounding voice.
  3. Optional: Use Expressive Avatar (paid) for more natural gestures and emotions.

Tip: If your script has multiple scenes, you can use the same avatar throughout or switch for variety. Keep it consistent for professional explainers.

Step 6: Paste Your Script and Generate the Video

  1. Paste your refined script into the text box
  2. If your script is long, use Split by Scene or manually break it into sections
  3. Set language if not English
  4. Click Generate or Create Video

Vidnoz will: - Sync the avatar's lip movements to the voice - Add background visuals if using templates - Process the video (usually 2–5 minutes depending on length)

Processing time: Expect 1–3 minutes for a 60–90 second video. Longer videos take longer.

Step 7: Review, Edit, and Export

  1. Preview the video in the Vidnoz editor
  2. Edit: Adjust timing, swap avatar or voice, change background if needed
  3. Export: Choose 720p (free) or 1080p (paid). Download as MP4

Quality check: - Does the avatar's lip-sync look natural? - Are there any mispronunciations? (Fix in script and regenerate if needed) - Is the pacing right? Trim or extend if necessary

You've just built your first AI content pipeline. Script → Copy.ai. Video → Vidnoz. Repeat.

Step 8: Repurpose and Scale

Once you have one video, scale the workflow:

  1. Batch scripts — Use Copy.ai to generate 5–10 scripts in one session
  2. Template in Vidnoz — Save your avatar + voice + style as a template for consistency
  3. Repurpose — Export the same script as different formats: 60s for TikTok/Reels, 90s for YouTube Shorts, full length for YouTube

Advanced: Use Copy.ai workflows to automate script generation from topic lists. Use Vidnoz's API (Business+ plans) for batch video production.

Troubleshooting

Issue Fix
Script too long for Vidnoz free Split into 2–3 shorter videos, or upgrade to Starter
Avatar looks robotic Try Expressive Avatar (paid) or a different avatar
Voice mispronounces a word Replace with a synonym or phonetic spelling in script
Video processing fails Check script for special characters; try shorter segment
Copy.ai hits word limit Upgrade to Chat plan or use free credits strategically

FAQ

Do I need a paid plan for Copy.ai to follow this tutorial?

No. The free plan (2,000 words/month) is enough for 5–10 short scripts. For regular production, the Chat plan ($24–29/mo) gives unlimited words.

Do I need a paid plan for Vidnoz?

The free plan works for testing (~16 seconds/day). For full videos (60–90+ seconds), the Starter plan ($19.99/mo yearly) is recommended.

Can I use a different AI writer instead of Copy.ai?

Yes. Any AI writer (ChatGPT, Claude, Jasper, etc.) works. The workflow is: script → Vidnoz. Copy.ai is recommended for its brand voice and workflow features.

How long does the full pipeline take?

First run: 45–60 minutes. Once familiar: 20–30 minutes per video.

Can I use my own voice in Vidnoz?

Yes. Voice cloning is available on Vidnoz Business plan ($56.99/mo yearly).
